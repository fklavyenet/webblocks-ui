Section heading

Use a named intro block when the section needs editorial rhythm.

`wb-section-heading` keeps the eyebrow, title, and supporting copy visually tied together. It works well above grids, articles, feature rows, and pricing comparisons.

`wb-content-columns` works for paired ideas: problem and solution, value and proof, summary and detail. It is a simple two-column rhythm without introducing another layout wrapper vocabulary.

`wb-content-stack` is the calmer single-column companion. Use it when the section still needs reading measure but should remain inside a broader marketing page rather than a dedicated prose shell.

These primitives are intentionally modest. The point is not to become a site builder. The point is to stop rewriting the same hero, footer, and section CSS every time a public-facing page appears in the product.

They also stay compatible with the existing component layer, so cards, buttons, badges, forms, and icons can be reused without a separate visual system.

Muted support card

Use `wb-card-muted` for side notes, secondary callouts, timeline entries, or supporting product detail.

Accent support card

Use `wb-card-accent` when the card should feel featured without becoming a full-width promo panel.
